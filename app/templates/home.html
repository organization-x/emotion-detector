What Is Sentiment Analysis?

Sentiment Analysis refers to a capability of Natural Language Processing (NLP), one of the fields with high prospects for artificial intelligence; NLP itself is a branch of computer science (and AI) that specifically encompasses human language. The term Sentiment Analysis is self-explanatory, with the implied functions of an AI of its kind being able to analyze the sentiment behind textual input, or the overall tone of a set quantity of (coherent) writing. Some examples of its direct application include:

  • Customer Feedback
  • Product Analysis
  • Market and Competitor Research
  • Our Data

Our Data

The data we're utilizing is a collection of customer input from three review-orientated websites: Yelp, Amazon, and IMDb, and two social media / forum websites: Twitter and Reddit. In total, 4,343,455 comments and reviews compose the foundation of our AI. All of our information has been obtained from open-source datasets on websites like Kaggle.

  • Amazon — 3,999,999
  • IMDb & Movie Reviews — 114,720
  • Twitter — 162,980
  • Yelp — 1,000

WHY DATA NEEDS CLEANING

Data Preprocessing

Before data can be used to train an AI, it must be prepared. This includes fixing text, tokenizing, and formatting it into a data table. Once preprocessing work has been completed, the AI can be trained. 

Sample of preprocessing
